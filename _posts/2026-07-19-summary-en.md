## [LG Monitors Silently Install Software via Windows Update](https://videocardz.com/newz/lg-monitors-silently-install-software-through-windows-update-without-user-consent) ⭐️ 9.0/10

LG monitors are silently installing software packages through Windows Update without user consent, as confirmed by Gamers Nexus using an LG UltraGear 34GX900A-B. The software installs automatically when a monitor is connected via HDMI, even if the user already owned an older LG monitor. This poses significant security and privacy risks as the software runs at boot with full system and internet access, without sandboxing. It undermines user trust and control over their own systems, affecting a large number of LG monitor owners. The software is delivered via Windows Update's driver distribution mechanism, which automatically installs manufacturer-provided packages. Users can disable this behavior via Group Policy or Device Installation Settings, but the default setting enables silent installation.

hackernews · baranul · Jul 18, 10:21 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48956688)

**Background**: Windows Update automatically delivers driver and software updates for hardware devices, including monitors, to ensure compatibility and functionality. However, this mechanism can be abused by manufacturers to push unrelated software without user consent. The issue highlights a broader problem with Windows' driver consent model, where users have limited control over what gets installed.

## [Rare Growth Disorder May Hold Key to Cancer Prevention](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c8jnlmek8reo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Researchers have found that individuals with Laron syndrome, a rare growth disorder, have a significantly lower incidence of cancer compared to the general population. This discovery could open new avenues for cancer prevention therapies by targeting the growth hormone/IGF-1 pathway, potentially benefiting millions at risk of cancer. Laron syndrome is caused by mutations in the growth hormone receptor gene, leading to IGF-1 deficiency. Affected individuals also show reduced risks of diabetes and increased longevity.

**Background**: Laron syndrome is an autosomal recessive disorder characterized by short stature and resistance to growth hormone. It affects about 250-350 people worldwide, with the largest group in Ecuador. The condition results in low IGF-1 levels despite high growth hormone levels.
